Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium

Input values in integers form, are used to count "p" and "q" values, by means of Hardy-Weinberg principle, the expected numbers of: homozygous, heterozygous and rare homozygous individuals are obtained. Observed and expected values are compared by the chi-square goodness of fit test.

If at the used level of significance, there are statistically important differences, application will count the fixation index - Fis, positive values may means reduction of heterozygosity caused by for example Wahlund effect, inbreed or genetic drift. Excess heterozygotes, caused by e.g.: breeding selection or bottleneck effect could be reasons of negative Fis values.

Chi square

Chi square test family is a statistical instruments which allows to examine the relation between variables. The chi square tests must be used with assumptions:

  1. test group should be sufficiently large
  2. The data should be maximally randomly drawn from a population
  3. values in cells are adequate when no more than 20% of the expected values are smaller than five and there are no cells with zero

Independence (Associations) chi square:

Research hypothesis:
H0: There is no association between variables.
Ha : There is an association between variables.

The expected values for independence chi square and chi square values are counted following formulas:

  • Eij = expected frequency for the ith row and jth columm.
  • Ti = summary in the ith row
  • Tj = summary in the jth column
  • N = all rows or columns summary
  • Oi – observed value in cell
  • Ei – expected values for cell
  • n – a set of all cells

If the values in cell do not fulfill third condition, application will use chi square with Yates's correction for continuity, following formula:

it is important to note that this correction is adequate for 2x2 contingency tables otherwise more accurate results may be obtained by combining columns or rows. Degrees of freedom are counted by: dof = (r − 1) * (c − 1)
r – number of rows
c – number of columns

Coefficient of contingency are measures of association provide a means of summarizing the size of the association between variables

Coefficient of contingency are counted following:

for 2x2 (1 dof) tables by means of phi coefficient

for tables with dof > 1 using Crammer`s V coefficient

Coefficient of contingency range 0.0 - 1.0 value

Goodness-of-fit chi square

Allows to examine the compliance of the observed and expected variables.

Research hypothesis:
H0: The observed values are equal to theoretical values (expected).
Ha : The observed values are not equal to theoretical values (expected).

Degrees of freedom are counted by:

dof = k − 1

Note.

"the total values must be the same for both observed and expected frequencies, expected frequencies less than 1 are not allowed, no more than 20% of expected values are less than 5."

PIC and H

Heterozygosity (H) is parameter indicating the average frequency of occurrence of heterozygous individuals. PIC as well as Heterozygosity (H) is a measure of polymorphism for a marker locus used in linkage analys.

PIC is counted for bi,- or multi,- allelic locus following formula:

Pi , Pj are the population frequency of the ith and jth allele (float format),
l – number of alleles Heterozygosity (H)

PIC for dominant marker in form of dichotomous variables (1/0), calculated using formulas:

PIC = 1− [f2 + (1− f)2]

Where fi is frequency of amplified marker (1) and (1 - fi) is frequency of  absence of the marker in study group (0).

for codminant marker PIC range 0.0-1.0

for dominant marker PIC range 0.0-0.5
