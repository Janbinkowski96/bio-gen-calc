Hardy–Weinberg equilibrium

Input values (number of homozygous, heterozygous and rare homozygous individuals) in integers form, are used to count alleles frequency - "p" and "q" values, by means of Hardy-Weinberg principle the expected numbers of: homozygous, heterozygous and rare homozygous individuals are obtained. Observed and expected values are compared by the chi-square goodness of fit test.

If at the used level of significance, there are statistically important differences, application will count the fixation index - Fis, positive values may means reduction of heterozygosity, caused by for example Wahlund effect, inbreed or genetic drift. While negativ Fis value means excess of heterozygotes, caused by for example: breeding selection or bottleneck effect.

Chi-square tests

Chi-square test family is a statistical instruments which allows to examine the relation between variables. The chi-square tests must be used with assumptions:

  1. test group must be sufficiently large.
  2. data should be maximally randomly drawn from a population.
  3. values in cells are adequate only when: (1) no more than 20% of the expected values are smaller than five; (2) there are no cells with zero.

Independence (Associations) chi-square:

Research hypothesis:
H0: There is no association between variables.
Ha : There is an association between variables.

Goodness-of-fit chi square

Allows to examine the compliance of the observed and expected values.

Research hypothesis:
H0: The observed values are equal to theoretical values (expected).
Ha : The observed values are not equal to theoretical values (expected).

Genetic distance

Genetic distance is a measure of differences between a pairs of populations, there are couple of methods allowing to estimation differences based on alleles frequency in examined loci, such as:

  1. Standard genetic distance (Nei, 1972).
  2. Geometric genetic distance (Nei, 1978).
  3. Genetic distance (Nei et al., 1983).

3) Genetic distance could be estimate based on short tandem repeats (STR) polymorphism (Nei and Takazaki, 1983). This model of genetic distance estimation was considered as more effective than other methods. It is calculated following formula:

  • D - standard genetic distance
  • D` - geometric genetic distance
  • DA - genetic distance based on STR polymorphism
  • jX- the sum of allele frequency squares in "i" locus in X population.
  • jY- the sum of allele frequency squares in "i" locus in Y population.
  • jXY- the sum of allele frequency product between X and Y population in "i" locus.
  • JX-the arithmetic or geometric (J`) mean (depending on the type of distance) the jX values for the population pair
  • JY- the arithmetic or geometric (J`) mean (depending on the type of distance) the jY values for the population pair
  • JXY- the arithmetic or geometric (J`) mean (depending on the type of distance) the jXY values for the population pair
  • Xij & Yij frequencies of "i" STR in "j" locus in pair popultaion X,Y
  • r - number of analyzed STR loci

The results of genetic distance estimation are presented in the form of distance matrix and dendrogram. The application offers the possibility of selecting several types of methods to construct dendrogram such as: unweighted pair group method with arithmetic mean (UPGMA), weighted pair group method with averaging (WMPGA), Centroid linkage clustering (UPGMC), weighted pair group centroid clustering (WPGMC), single-linkage clustering and Complete-linkage clustering. The construction of dendrograms is performed by the Python library "scipy.cluster.hierarchy.linkage" lib documentation and theorethical information are availbe from: https://docs.scipy.org/doc/scipy/reference/generated/scipy.cluster.hierarchy.linkage.html#scipy.cluster.hierarchy.linkage
